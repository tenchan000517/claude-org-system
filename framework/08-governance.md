# Governance

How direction-level decisions get made, recorded, and honored. Light for a solo operator (you and a
decision log); fuller for a multi-department org. Take what fits.

## Resolutions of record

Direction-level decisions (not day-to-day work) are made deliberately and written down as
**resolutions**: what was decided, why, when, and who must comply. A resolution log
(`templates/governance/resolution-log.md`) is the org's memory of "what we've already settled" — it
stops decisions being silently reversed or re-argued.

Once recorded, a resolution binds: roles follow settled decisions rather than relitigating them each
session. (Disagree? Raise it to be re-decided — don't just ignore it.)

## Strategy review (periodic, optional)

On a cadence, leads come together (or one operator sits down) to review direction: what's working,
what's missed, what to change. Output is a set of resolutions, captured in the log and reflected in
each department's targets. Template: `templates/governance/strategy-meeting-notes.md`.

## Executive accountability

The executive/leadership layer owns the org's top-level outcome. Concretely:

- **Owns the KGI** — accountable for the headline result, not just activity.
- **Sets and revises direction** — and records the change as a resolution.
- **Makes go / no-go / withdrawal calls** — including proposing to stop a losing effort (see the
  failure/withdrawal judgment in `06-judgment.md`).
- **Receives reports** — department leads report up in a fixed format
  (`templates/reports/monthly-report.md`) so performance is comparable over time.

## Reporting cadence

A fixed reporting format makes periods comparable and forces an honest "planned vs actual." The cadence
(monthly is common) and the format are set once and kept stable. Solo operators often skip formal
reporting — the map + handoff already carry "where things stand."

## The minimal version

If you're one person: a **resolution/decision log** in the secretary, plus the session-lifecycle
handoff, *is* your governance. Don't build meeting machinery for an audience of one. Scale up only when
multiple accountable parties actually need to coordinate.
