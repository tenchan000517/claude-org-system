# Departments (multi-domain model)

For organizations past one person/one domain. A department is a **scoped domain with its own
accountability**. Solo setups skip this entirely — they have one worker space and a leader.

## A department = a scoped domain

Each department:

- owns one **domain** (a coherent area of work),
- has a **lead** accountable for its outcome,
- runs its work in its own **directory/space** (so context and memory scope to it),
- derives a **working document** from the credo (`templates/department.CLAUDE.md`), and
- exposes its **status** up to the org map.

## Targets: KGI / KPI (or just "definition of done")

A department names what success means for it:

- **KGI** — the goal-level outcome it owns (the "are we winning?" number/state).
- **KPI** — the leading indicators it manages week to week.

Small/internal departments can replace these with a plain *definition of done*. The point is that each
department can answer "what am I accountable for, and how do I know I'm hitting it?"

## Profit vs cost centers (a useful generic distinction)

Departments differ in how they relate to the org's top-level goal:

- **Profit/impact center** — directly moves the org's headline outcome.
- **Cost/enabling center** — doesn't directly move it, but enables those that do (infrastructure,
  support, internal tooling).

Naming this prevents two mistakes: judging an enabling department by a headline number it can't move,
and letting an impact department hide behind activity that doesn't move the number.

## Cross-department orchestration

- The **org-level map** (an INDEX at the top) lists departments, their domains, leads, and status —
  the same pattern as a leader's project map, one level up.
- Work that spans departments is coordinated at that level, with the decision recorded as a resolution
  (`framework/08-governance.md`), not buried in one department's memory.
- Shared infrastructure/information lives in the **secretary** so departments don't each re-derive it.

## Department boundaries (stay in your lane)

A department does not wander into another's domain on its own initiative. Cross-domain ideas are raised
as a proposal at the org level — they are a separate, explicit effort, not a self-authorized expansion.
This is the credo's "minimal interpretation" applied to organizational scope.
